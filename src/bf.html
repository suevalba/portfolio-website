Brain Flash

An App to Learn a New Vocabulary

Project Name: Brain Flash
My Role: Solo Project
About the project: A vocabulary app that uses flash cards
What is unique about this project: It is fast, simple and intuitive and powerful way to quickly learn new words that can be used wherever you are to practice your personalized flash cards. Users can methodically categorize, reference, and study new vocabulary and concepts so they can move forward confidently.

Understanding the Problem

User Story

As a special education teacher, I want to practice my British sign language skills and Maori language so that I can communicate better with my students and their parents.
As a wife, I want to start a family and be a mom
As a fresh graduate of I.T. Management, I want to move to the city so that I could get a job in the I.T. Industry and practice what I've learned.

Job Story

When on of my students expresses something, I want to respond to him/her accordingly so I can communicate with them in a way that we both understand.
When I move to the city, I want to make sure I am ready for my new career in I.T. so I can adjust to my new environment faster.
When I go to work, I want to explore nature and observe the city during my commute so I can be more familiar with the things around me.

Problem and Hypothesis Statement

Problem Statement

Grace needs a way to adapt to her new environment by understanding the culture and language so she can communicate fluently to the people from all ages.

Hypothesis Statement

We believe that by building a feature where the mobile app can respond to your sentences and expressions will help Grace practice and learn more about the language and figure out if her pronunciation is correct.

Learning Challenges

  • Think about the concept first, then design later.
  • Keep a copy of all your sketches, from Crazy 8s, to prototypes because you’ll never know when you need to go back to those ideas and use them.
  • Take note and value all the feedback from your user testers, but you don’t need to follow all of them.
  • The whole process in this project makes me practice myself putting my shoes on the user’s experience.
  • Getting users to test your prototype and setting an appointment with them is very challenging. It's best to widen network and ask help for friends too.
