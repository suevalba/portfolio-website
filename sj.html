Schnitzeljagd

The Social Scavenger Hunting App

Project Name: Schnitzeljagd
My Role: Solo Project from scratch to final iteration
About the project:Schnitzeljagd is a social scavenger hunting app for real objects and current locations.
What is unique about this project: Users can play social scavenger hunting with friends through social sharing.
Challenges about this project: I don't have any experience in social scavenging or any type of game related to scavenger hunting. It is challenging to start creating an app I am not familiar with.

Understanding the Problem

Problem Statement

“Gamers need a way to enjoy social scavenger games for real objects because they want to play social interactive games with friends even when on the go”

Problems and Solutions

Possible Problems

  • Can be time consuming to set up since it needs to be tailored to an accurate address and neighborhood
  • Needs to be scaled to any supported location and involved friends through social sharing
  • Possible issue on accuracy of the user's location when on the move
  • How does it support rural areas that do not have access to internet
  • With GPS and mobile data turned on, it could consume a lot of battery while playing

Possible Solutions

  • The app can support auto complete to easily fill out needed information with less time
  • Activate GPS on phones and share location to the players within the group
  • Players are encouraged to download offline GPS mode for a more accurate location if GPS signal gets lost
  • Players will have an option for battery saving mode where they can play with dark screen, or use Wi-Fi when present and/or enable offline GPS

Surveys and Interviews

The surveys and interviews determines the behaviors and general attitude of the users. In this app, it also includes identifying their attitude towards social media and how it influences their daily lives. These goals will help me discover how users will enjoy social scavenger hunting and uncover possible problems they could encounter during the game. Google Forms is a helpful tool to analyze their responses.

Insights

  • Top social media platforms are Facebook, Instagram, and YouTube. The main purpose of their social media platforms are to connect with friends, family, co-workers, for learning and discovering, and lastly for entertainment and games.
  • Since the participants are active on social media, I think playing Schnitzeljagd that aims to play with other players will be successful except there’s a little chance to play with strangers in the game.
  • What participants enjoy the most in scavenger hunting is mostly trying to solve riddles, finding clues discovering unknown places while having fun with friends
  • The most difficult part of playing was if they couldn’t understand the questions, they wouldn't be able to find the clues, so they won’t be able to solve the riddles and finish the game
  • Participants enjoyed scavenger hunting when solving riddles together with friends. They begin to discover places that they see everyday but never thought it actually exist in their everyday lives. The experience that they have, the funny moments and memories with friends brought a sense of accomplishment and working with a team is the most fun part of the game.

User Research Analysis

After collecting data from user interviews, sticky notes helped me organize and record interesting behaviors, needs, frustrations, quotes, and facts.

Insights

  • User wants rare treasures to be found not just in one location
  • User wants more specific instructions so it is easier to follow
  • User wants events that are available to players who misses the event
  • User wishes to have a directional arrow point to follow the map and find clues
  • User wants to level up or enable skill enhancements in catching clues and solving riddles
  • User wants a variety of tasks
  • User wants to solve riddles and find items in a safer environment and not just in random places

Findings

  • Some treasures are location limited
  • Vague clues make me walk around cluelessly
  • Some events are time limited. I miss the event when I don’t have time
  • I hope they add more specific clues
  • I wish there’s a navigation arrow point
  • It’s frustrating to see the same Pokémon all the time
  • I wish there’s a skill to level up in throwing poke ball to avoid wasting it
  • I just want to catch the good ones, but it takes too long to catch
  • It gets boring when it becomes repetitive
  • I was concerned of my safety because I find Pokémons in unsafe places in the neighborhood

Solutions

  • When a rare treasure is to be found, we need to place several items in places and consider the number of players or users in a specific location. We shouldn’t concentrate in just one specific location the world
  • In providing instructions, adding a directional arrow is more efficient for the players to follow the map
  • Instructions and clues should be clear and simple
  • Special events can be done at least 24 hours depending on the time zone to accommodate other players who are not available on that time period
  • Create clues and solutions according to skills of the players
  • Tasks should be updated and changed from time to time
  • Ensure safety of the players by getting feedback from other players about the area

Usability Testing

Type of Test: Moderated Remote
Audio-Video Recording Used: Lookback
About the project:Schnitzeljagd is a social scavenger hunting app for real objects and current locations.
Hardware Tools Used: Laptop Computer, Android Phone, Headset with Mic
Challenges: Due to pandemic and limited accessibility, it was difficult to gather testers in a short period of time. Some the participants are also coming from different continents which makes the time zone different too.

It's time to put my design to test! Using my Mid-Fidelity Wireframes, I worked with 6 participants to gather their thoughts and frustrations on Schnitzeljagd. The goals are to:

  • Determine the behaviors ang general attitude of participants towards the game
  • Identify attitudes towards social media and how it influences them in their daily lives
  • Discover how players enjoy the social scavenger hunts
  • Uncover problems they encounter during the game

Conclusion

  • Most of the participants are confused on how to use the Search box and the purpose of the Search box in the map. The main purpose of the Search box is to type an exact address where the participant wants to go or if the participant is going to a different address which is outside the neighborhood. Some think it is for finding friends in the game.
  • Tutorials button should be eliminated in once the participant is logged in. It can be added in the menu so participant can go back to it only when needed
  • Using the gems as an symbol for treasure is effective
  • Players and Invites feature can be in one place
  • Adding a Share button is a good idea to invite more friends
  • Adding a short description on the profile is more effective in adding friends
  • Create a point system to unlock other features in the game
  • Activity settings should be tick boxes for a more detailed information on how to choose the right activity and tailored to the capacity of the players

Usability Results and Planning for Future Tests

Prototypes are meant to be perfected. I used Affinity Mapping and Rainbow Spreadsheet in methodically sorting out the results of my Usability Tests. I paid special attention to errors and their emotions while finishing a task.

Key Findings and Iterations

After considering what changes and improvements should be done for the users, I categorized which issue have the highest severity and which ones have low severity. I chose the top 5 most critical errors that needed iterations.

  1. Create a separate search box in the map
  2. Place the Invites button in the Players button to approve, block, report or ignore game friend requests. Players can also view existing friends or search for a specific name in the game.
  3. Add checkboxes for specific needs and requirements of the player
  4. Change distance settings to slider
  5. Change the design of Social subtitle
